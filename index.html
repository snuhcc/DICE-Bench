DICE-BENCH: Evaluating the Tool-Use Capabilities of Large Language Models in Multi-Round, Multi-Party Dialogues

Kyochul Jang1, Donghyeon Lee1, Kyusik Kim1, Dongseok Heo1, Taewhoo Lee1, Woojeong Kim1, Bongwon Suh1
1Seoul National University
Paper arXiv Code Data

DICE-BENCH is the first benchmark to evaluate LLMs' ability to use tools in realistic multi-round, multi-party group chat scenarios.

Abstract

We present DICE-BENCH, a novel benchmark designed to evaluate the tool-use capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in multi-round, multi-party dialogues—a challenging scenario that mirrors real-world group chat environments.

Our benchmark synthesizes realistic group conversations with 2-4 participants across 4 rounds, creating complex scenarios where tool-calling cues are dispersed throughout the dialogue. We introduce DICE-SCORE, a metric that quantifies dialogue difficulty by measuring the dispersion of tool-related information, with higher scores indicating more challenging inputs.

Our evaluation reveals that even state-of-the-art models like GPT-4o achieve only 64% exact match accuracy on average, with performance degrading as the number of rounds or participants increases. This highlights a significant gap in current LLMs' ability to handle complex, multi-party tool-use scenarios. As the first benchmark to combine multi-round multi-party dialogue with inter-tool dependencies, DICE-BENCH provides a comprehensive evaluation framework with fully open code, data, and pipeline.

Method Overview

DICE-BENCH synthesizes realistic group chat scenarios with natural turn-taking and context-dependent tool requirements. Our data generation pipeline creates authentic multi-party conversations where tool-calling cues are naturally dispersed throughout the dialogue.

DICE-SCORE Metric

We introduce DICE-SCORE, a novel metric that quantifies input difficulty by measuring how tool-related information is dispersed throughout the dialogue. Higher scores indicate more challenging scenarios where models must maintain context across multiple rounds and participants.

Performance Analysis

Model Performance Across Rounds

Our evaluation shows consistent performance degradation as dialogue complexity increases. All models struggle particularly with 4-round, 4-party scenarios, highlighting the challenge of maintaining context and extracting tool-use requirements from complex multi-party interactions.

Inter-tool Dependencies

DICE-BENCH includes complex scenarios requiring multiple tools with dependencies, testing LLMs' ability to understand and execute multi-step tool chains. This reflects real-world scenarios where tools must be called in specific sequences with outputs feeding into subsequent calls.

Dataset Statistics

DICE-BENCH contains 1,607 carefully curated dialogues across various domains, with balanced distribution across different numbers of participants and rounds. Each dialogue is annotated with ground-truth tool calls and DICE-SCORE values.

Dialogue Distribution
